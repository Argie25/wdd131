Normal/Gray Cockatiels

Normal Gray is the most common and the original Cockatiel first found in Australia

M1 Pied Cockatiels

There are no distinguishable markings between Male and Female Pied birds. There can be a lot of light or white, "a heavy Pied" or they can just have yellow on the back of their head, "a split pied".

M2 Lutino Cockatiels

Lutino babies, Females, and adult males all look alike. If you look close, you may be able to see strpes on the tale feathers of the babies and the Females that will not be on the adult males. Both are born with red eyes.

M3 PEARL/Oplaine/Laced Cockatiels

Adult male cockaCockatiels lose their pearl markings at about one year in age. At this time they look like a normal gray bird. The Females will keep the pearl markings for their lifetime .

M4 Cinnamon/Isablle/
Fawn Cockatiels

Cinnamon CockaCockatiels are just like normal grays, but a lighter color. The Females look like the babies.

M5 Recessive Silver/RS Cockatiels

Recessive Silver CockaCockatiels have red eyes, and must have a split silver or visual silver gen in both parents.

M6 Fallow Cockatiels

Male Fallows are darker than the Females. The male looks almost like a cinnamon and the Female looks like a lutino. They all have red eyes.

M7 White Face/Charcoal WF Cockatiels

White Face Cockatiels have no yellow or orange color in their feather.

M8 Dominant Silver Cockatiels

Dominant Silver will appear paler, with a brownish wash to their plumage. Their eyes are brown and the beak and legs are charcoal to black.

M9 Pastel Face/PF Cockatiels

Pastel Face Cockatiels have a peach colored cheek patch.

M10 Sex-Linked Yellowcheek Cockatiels

In order to get visual SLYC of both sexes both parents must carry the gene.

M11 Dominant Yellow Cheek/
DYC Cockatiels

Some DYC Cockatiels have a much lighter cheek patch and some look like pastel face Cockatiels. They are easy to ID, if they are the light yellow color.

M12 Gold Cheek Cockatiels

M13 Emerald/Olive/Dilute Suffused Yellow or
Silver Cockatiels

M14 Creamface Cockatiels

Creamfaces visually looks like a combination of Whiteface and Pastelface.

Pewter Cockatiel

The Pewter Cockatiel Mutation is exclusive to Australia Lighter than normal grey but the tone is darker than cinnamon and the feet are much darker compared to the cinnamon.
